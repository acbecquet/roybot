0 · Lock the open decisions

Spec §10 left these open "settle in Phase 2." They drive what you order, so do them first.

  • Hard floors → 2 drive wheels + 1 caster/ball. Carpet → 4-wheel tank for grip. This changes the wheel order below.

  • 2 DC motors draw far less than the old 4-servo plan. Pick 1S or 2S LiPo + motor voltage. This couples to the dock charger (#6): 1S→TP4056, 2S→BMS.

  • Speed vs torque balance for a small fast cat-toy. Higher RPM = darty; more torque = won't stall on carpet/edges.

  • Dedicated buck regulator + bulk cap so motor surges can't brown out the Pi (spec §4A). Size it for Pi Zero 2 W + peripherals.

1 · Procure the BOM

Spec §5 deltas vs GrowBot. Order everything here in one go — this is the shipping wait.

  • Encoders are required (closed-loop speed + odometry for docking later). Use the ratio from §0.

  • Chosen over DRV8833 for higher efficiency at this scale.

  • Match the floor decision. Caster/ball for hard floors; 4-wheel tank for carpet.

  • Feather/pom on a short (~10–20 cm) rigid or braided arm — no free end, internally over-molded. No loose string, ever.

  • Plus the matching charger/charge-management: 1S TP4056 or 2S BMS.

  • Isolates the Pi rail from motor current spikes.

  • Heat-set inserts / screws for a tool-locked battery hatch (no swallowable parts). Integral color or fully-cured non-toxic finish.

  • Plus heat-shrink. Fuse/inline protection on the battery lead.

  • Pi Zero 2 W, MPU-6050 IMU, OV5647 cam, WS2812 ring, MAX98357A amp + speaker, INMP441 mic, microSD.

  • Removed vs GrowBot: 2× SCS0009 servos, 1 kΩ resistor, MT3608 boost. The servo bus is gone.

  • Soldering iron, multimeter, calipers, 3D-printer access.

2 · Print the chassis (PETG)

Buildable while parts ship — model/print around the motors + battery you chose.

  • Low CG so it can't easily flip (or shaped to self-right). Size to the actual parts, not guesses.

  • Compliant mounts for the PCB/battery so play impacts don't crack solder joints.

  • Every moving-part gap <4–6 mm or >25 mm — never 4–25 mm. Shroud the wheels; round all edges.

  • Nothing detachable fits the 31.7 × 57.1 mm small-parts cylinder. Captive/recessed/glued fasteners; tool-locked battery hatch.

3 · Wire & electronics bring-up

Bench-test on the desk before anything goes in the chassis.

  • From GrowBot's pin map. The serial servo layer is replaced by H-bridge + encoder GPIO.

  • Bulk cap across the Pi rail. Keep motor ground and logic ground joined at one point. Inline fuse on the battery.

  • Forward + reverse, one motor at a time, chassis off. Confirm direction and that nothing browns out the Pi.

  • Reuse leds.py, audio.py, camera.py. Mic + cam get used by sub-projects #2/#5 later — just verify they're alive now.

4 · Driver bring-up on hardware

Port the closed-form sim driver to real PWM. The sim mapping is differential_drive(v_fwd, v_yaw).

  • Same kinematics as the sim driver, output to the TB6612FNG. Keep per-motor calibration knobs (gain/deadband) — the physical world needs tuning the model can't see.

  • Real motors aren't matched; trim so "straight" actually goes straight.

  • Straight = equal wheels, spin = opposite, scaling monotonic (success criterion #6: drives forward + turns in place).

  • Add a closed-loop speed PI only if open-loop tracking is too sloppy. Odometry helps the dock approach (#3) later.

5 · Deploy the policy (sim2real)

The tiny MLP runs on the Pi at 50 Hz. Close the sim-to-real gap before trusting it near Roy.

  • Prereq (still pending from Phase 1.5): scripts/train.py --timesteps 1500000 --out runs/smart_policy, then export + scripts/view.py runs/smart_policy.npz. Watch for camping (near-zero robot displacement = regression).

  • Torch-free inference, sub-ms. The Pi must frame-stack (N_STACK = 6) — same as the env, per infer.py's note.

  • PWM→speed with deadband + saturation; inject 10–30 ms latency (Tan et al. 2018). Re-train/fine-tune so the policy expects real dynamics.

  • Mass, friction, latency, motor-gain ranges should straddle your measured values. Widen if the real bot sits at an edge.

  • Against a hand-held lure first (no cat). Confirm it moves sensibly, then tune.

6 · Safety-floor reflexes (always-on)

Spec §4E + §9.7 — these run under the policy and override it, instantly, no LLM.

  • Also a current/stall cutoff so a jammed wheel can't cook a motor.

  • Provoke a stall, a tip, an edge on purpose and watch it react (success criterion #7).

7 · Cat-safety sign-off — GATE before Roy

Non-negotiable (spec §6). All boxes here must be checked before Roy meets the bot.

  • Loose string is the #1 fatal hazard (linear foreign body). Attended-play only; store after use. If string is ever seen from mouth/rear: vet same-day, do not pull.

8 · Real chase validation

The Phase 2 finish line — the consent ethic has to survive contact with a real cat.

  • Watch for over-chase/pester. It should back off when she's done. Stop if she looks stressed. This is the whole point.

  • Until #2 exists, position/engagement are hand-fed or scripted. #2 replaces them with real tracking.
